New York Style / Kosher Dill

New York is well-known for its iconic foods, including old-fashioned kosher dill pickles. These kinds of pickles are a staple in delis and street carts all over the city and have been enjoyed for generations.

In the pickle world, the word “kosher” refers to the use of garlic in the pickling brine, not necessarily the adherence to Jewish dietary laws. The use of garlic in pickling is an age-old tradition, and it adds a dimension of flavor that sets kosher dill pickles apart from other pickles.

Fermentation

We take great pride in using traditional methods to create the perfect pickle. Unlike most supermarket pickles (which are boiled and preserved in a salt or vinegar solution), our kosher dill pickles are made using a natural lacto-fermentation process (sv: mjölksyrning). This results in a more crisp, crunchy texture and a complex tangy, briny flavor.

What’s more, fermented pickles are packed with probiotics, meaning that there’s plenty of good bacteria that contribute to a healthy gut microbiome.

Cucumbers

You can pickle all kinds of cucumbers.

In Sweden, the most common type of cucumbers are English cucumbers (sv: slanggurkor). These are long, thin-skinned cucumbers with a mild flavor, commonly available in supermarkets year-round and sold in plastic wrap to maintain freshness and reduce food waste. You can slice these cucumbers in quarters for making pickle spears.

Västerås cucumbers (sv: västeråsgurkor) are a type of small, firm cucumber that is typically grown outdoors during the summer months. Västerås cucumbers are great for pickling but are mostly only available from July to September.
