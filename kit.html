Let Me Tell You About Cucumbers

Alright I’m about to drop some cucumber knowledge on you. For pickling, you can use all types of cucumbers, but it’s sure worth it to get your hands on the freshest ones possible.

Now, in Sweden, you’re most likely to come across English cucumbers (sv: slanggurkor). These long, thin-skinned cucumbers are available year-round in the supermarket and are wrapped in plastic to keep them fresh and reduce food waste. They work really well for making pickle spears, so go ahead and slice them up.

But if it’s the summer, you can try to get a hold of Västerås cucumbers (sv: västeråsgurkor). These small, firm cucumbers are grown outdoors, and they’re perfect for pickling. The only catch is that they’re only available from July to September.
