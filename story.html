So, once upon a time, in a land (Sweden) far from home (America), I found myself in a real pickle (pun intended). I was craving the taste of old-fashioned New York-style kosher dill pickles, but all the pickles I could find were too salty (sv: saltgurkor) or too sweet and vinegary (sv: ättiksgurkor). I mean, you cannot just eat those things! So, I decided to take matters into my own hands and make proper kosher dill pickles, the kind I used to snack on when I was a kid.

For a long time, I worked on perfecting the recipe, trying to get that just-right crunch and the perfect balance of ingredients that make kosher dill pickles so delicious (dill-icious?). And after a ton of trial and error, I finally did it! I made a pickle that was worthy of the “New York” name. It had the perfect crunch, tangy flavor, and the right balance of dill, garlic, salt, and spices.

I was so proud of my creation that I knew I had to share it with the world (at least Stockholm). And that’s how Pickle John’s Old-Fashioned New York Style Kosher Dill Pickles was born.


“I didn’t choose the pickle life, the pickle life chose me.” — P.J.
